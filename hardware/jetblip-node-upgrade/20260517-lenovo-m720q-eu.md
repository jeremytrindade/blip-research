## Why the M720q? — The PCIe Riser Advantage

The M720q Tiny is the only Mini PC in its price class with an internal PCIe slot (proprietary riser `01AJ929`). This allows adding a quad-NIC, 10GbE card, or extra NVMe adapter — something no HP, Dell, or N100 machine offers. Combined with i5-8500T (6C/6T, 35W TDP), 64GB DDR4 ceiling, and NVMe + SATA native, it is the logical "production server" choice over the Pi 5.

**PCIe Riser note:** Rarely included in refurbished units. Buy separately on eBay/AliExpress (~€15–40, search `M720q PCIe Riser 01AJ929`). Installing it occupies the 2.5" SATA bay — only one storage device available when riser is used.

## Alternative Context

> Source: Reddit r/minilab — "Used Mini PC Difficulties: Lenovo vs HP vs Dell"
> https://www.reddit.com/r/minilab/comments/1hv9sry/used_mini_pc_difficulties_lenovo_vs_hp_vs_dell/

Community consensus on reliability for refurbished Mini PCs:
- **Lenovo ThinkCentre Tiny:** Best thermal design and internal access. Proprietary fan connectors (4/5 pin) can complicate replacement but parts are available.
- **HP EliteDesk Mini:** Clean design, often includes USB-C. BIOS sometimes rejects third-party hardware (RAM/NIC).
- **Dell Optiplex Micro:** Most parts available on used market. Some generations use proprietary 2.5" caddies.

> Source: Alex Ellis — "I Bought An N100 Mini PC, Then Another" (Aug 2025)
> https://blog.alexellis.io/n100-mini-computer/

N100 Geekbench 6: 1,226 single / 3,345 multi — vs Pi 5: 777 single / 1,496 multi. The N100 is 1.6x faster single-core and 2.2x faster multi-core. Runs KVM/Firecracker microVMs. However: CPU is soldered, typically single-channel RAM, no internal expansion beyond M.2. Best used as a secondary efficiency node, not as a primary modular server.
